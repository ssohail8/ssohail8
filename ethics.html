Ethical Considerations for Data Scientists

As data scientists, our decisions can impact many areas. We are committed to handling data ethically:

  • Responsible Data Management: We take care of data from the moment we collect it until we no longer need it. We have clear rules on how to handle data and we stick to these rules.
  • Privacy and Confidentiality: Keeping data private is crucial. We use methods that keep personal information safe and prevent unauthorized access.
  • Avoiding Bias: We constantly check our work to avoid any unfair bias in our data or results. We want to make sure our outcomes are fair and helpful.
  • Transparency and Accountability: We are open about how we work and the limitations of our methods. Being transparent helps others understand and trust our work, ensuring that decisions are made based on accurate information.

The Federal Data Strategy Data Ethics Framework guides us to make decisions that respect privacy, ensure accountability, and are transparent, thus building trust in what we do.
